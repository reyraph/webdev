Demo 7 â€” Image Cropping

object-fit  |  object-position  |  overflow: hidden on the container

A â€” object-fit values

All five boxes are the same size (260 Ã— 180 px) and use the same image. Only object-fit changes.

  • object-fit: fill

    Stretches the image to fill the box exactly. Aspect ratio is not preserved â€” the image distorts.

  • object-fit: contain

    Scales the image to fit entirely inside the box. No cropping, no distortion â€” letterbox bars appear if ratios differ.

  • object-fit: cover

    Scales up until the box is fully covered, then crops the overflow. Most used for card thumbnails.

  • object-fit: none

    Renders the image at its natural size, ignoring the box dimensions. May overflow or leave gaps.

  • object-fit: scale-down

    Picks whichever result is smaller: none or contain. Never enlarges beyond natural size.

B â€” object-position anchors

All six boxes use object-fit: cover on the same tall portrait image. Only the crop anchor (object-position) changes â€” controlling which part of the image stays visible.

  • object-position: top left

    Anchors the crop to the upper-left corner of the image.

  • object-position: top center

    Shows the top strip of the image, centred horizontally. Useful for hero shots where the subject is at the top.

  • object-position: center

    The browser default. The crop is taken from the exact centre of the image.

  • object-position: bottom center

    Anchors the crop to the bottom strip. Good for images where the subject stands at the bottom.

  • object-position: bottom right

    Anchors the crop to the lower-right corner of the image.

  • object-position: 25% 75%

    Custom percentage values: 25 % from the left edge and 75 % from the top. Any unit (px, %, em) is valid.
